Canine Vaccination Procedure

Abstract: Step-by-step instructions for administering vaccines to dogs, ensuring safety and compliance with veterinary guidelines.

Prerequisites

  • Dog’s medical history reviewed
  • Owner consent obtained
  • Vaccines and syringes prepared
  • Proper PPE (gloves, lab coat)

Context

This task is intended for veterinary technicians and veterinarians in small animal clinics. Applicable for routine puppy vaccinations and boosters.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Vaccine

    Check vial expiration → Draw correct dose into syringe

    Info: Use aseptic technique to prevent contamination.

  2. Restrain the Dog

    Use gentle but firm restraint to prevent sudden movements.

    Example: Puppy on table, assistant supports head and body, technician prepares injection site.

  3. Administer Injection

    Inject subcutaneously between shoulder blades

    Dog remains calm, no immediate adverse reaction.

  4. Record Vaccination

    Update medical record with vaccine type, lot number, and date administered.

    Important for regulatory compliance and booster reminders.

  5. Observe Post-Vaccination

    Monitor dog for 15–30 minutes for allergic reactions.

    Emergency kit must be nearby in case of anaphylaxis.

Expected Result

Dog is successfully vaccinated, documented in medical records, and owner informed of follow-up.

Post-Requisites

  • Schedule next booster according to vaccination schedule
  • Dispose of sharps and medical waste properly
  • Clean and disinfect preparation area
